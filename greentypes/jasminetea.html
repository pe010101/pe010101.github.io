Jasmine Tea

✢ ✢

Jasmine tea (茉莉花茶) is a famous scented tea from China, renowned for its delicate floral aroma and sweet, refreshing taste. Created through an intricate process of layering tea leaves with fresh jasmine blossoms, this aromatic tea represents one of the most beloved and widely consumed floral teas in the world, balancing the health benefits of tea with the enchanting fragrance of jasmine flowers.

‿̩͙⊱༒︎━━━━━━﹙History and Origin﹚━━━━━━༒︎⊰‿̩͙

Song Dynasty Beginnings
Jasmine tea originated during the Song Dynasty (960-1279 AD) in the Fujian province of China. The art of scenting tea with flowers developed as Chinese tea masters sought to create more aromatic and complex tea experiences. Jasmine was chosen for its intensely fragrant blossoms that bloom at night, making them ideal for the scenting process that traditionally occurs during evening hours.
Ming Dynasty Refinement
During the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644), jasmine tea production techniques were refined and standardized. The process of layering tea leaves with fresh jasmine blossoms was perfected, and jasmine tea became increasingly popular among the scholar-official class. The tea's delicate aroma and refined character made it particularly appealing to intellectuals and artists who appreciated its subtle beauty.
Qing Dynasty Imperial Recognition
Jasmine tea gained imperial favor during the Qing Dynasty (1644-1912), particularly under the Qianlong Emperor who was known for his appreciation of fine teas. The tea became a tribute tea sent to the imperial court, cementing its status as one of China's premier scented teas. Production expanded beyond Fujian to other regions including Guangxi and Yunnan.
Global Popularization
In the 19th and early 20th centuries, jasmine tea became one of the most famous Chinese tea exports to the West. Its approachable floral character appealed to Western palates unfamiliar with traditional Chinese teas. Today, jasmine tea remains one of the most recognized and widely consumed Chinese teas internationally, enjoyed both for its flavor and aromatic qualities.

‿̩͙⊱༒︎━━━━━━﹙Production Process﹚━━━━━━༒︎⊰‿̩͙

Base Tea Selection
Jasmine tea typically uses green tea as its base, though white tea and sometimes black or oolong teas are also used. The base tea is processed in spring and stored until summer when jasmine flowers bloom. High-quality jasmine tea uses tender spring tea buds that can better absorb the jasmine fragrance. The tea must have good porosity and neutral character to serve as an effective scent carrier.
Jasmine Harvest Timing
Jasmine flowers are harvested during the day in summer when they are still closed buds. The flowers are kept cool and transported carefully to processing facilities where they begin to open as evening approaches. The timing is crucial - flowers must be at the perfect stage of opening to release their maximum fragrance during the scenting process that occurs through the night.
Layering and Scenting
The core process involves alternating layers of tea leaves and jasmine blossoms in specialized containers. As the flowers open through the night, they release their essential oils which are absorbed by the tea leaves. The containers are kept at controlled temperatures and periodically turned to ensure even scenting. This process may continue for 4-6 hours before the spent flowers are removed.
Multiple Scenting Cycles
Premium jasmine tea undergoes multiple scenting cycles over several days or even weeks. Each night, fresh jasmine blossoms replace the spent flowers from the previous cycle. Higher grades may undergo 5-9 separate scentings, with the highest quality "Yin Hao" jasmine tea receiving seven or more scentings. This repeated process creates exceptionally fragrant and complex tea.

‿̩͙⊱༒︎━━━━━━﹙Types and Classification﹚━━━━━━༒︎⊰‿̩͙

Scenting Grade Categories
Yin Hao (Silver Tip): The highest grade, using silver needle white tea buds with 7+ scentings, featuring exceptional fragrance and subtlety
Dragon Phoenix Pearl: Hand-rolled pearls that unfurl when brewed, typically receiving 5-7 scentings for balanced aroma
Standard Jasmine: Good quality jasmine green tea with 3-5 scentings, offering classic jasmine character at affordable prices
Base Tea Varieties
Jasmine Green Tea: The most common type, using Chinese green tea as base, offering fresh vegetal notes complemented by floral aroma
Jasmine White Tea: Using white tea buds for a more delicate base that allows the jasmine to shine more prominently
Jasmine Black Tea: Less common, using black tea for a stronger base that stands up to the floral notes
Jasmine Oolong: Using partially oxidized oolong tea for complex base flavors that interact with jasmine aroma
Form Factors
Loose Leaf: Traditional form allowing full appreciation of leaf appearance and aroma
Pearls/Balls: Hand-rolled tea balls that dramatically unfurl when steeped
Blooming/Flowering: Artistic teas that "bloom" into flower shapes when steeped, often containing actual jasmine flowers
Tea Bags: Convenient form for quick preparation, though typically using lower-grade materials

‿̩͙⊱༒︎━━━━━━﹙Flavor Profile and Characteristics﹚━━━━━━༒︎⊰‿̩͙

Visual Appearance
High-quality jasmine tea features well-preserved tea leaves, often with visible silver tips in premium grades. The leaves should have a uniform appearance with a slight sheen from absorbed jasmine oils. When brewed, the liquor displays a pale yellow to light amber color, depending on the base tea used. Jasmine pearls dramatically unfurl into complete leaves, creating a beautiful visual spectacle.
Aroma Complexities
The dry leaf aroma presents an intense jasmine fragrance that should be fresh and natural rather than perfumey or artificial. High-quality jasmine tea has a complex aroma with notes of honey, sweet flowers, and sometimes subtle fruity undertones. When brewed, the aroma expands to include the base tea's characteristics while maintaining the prominent jasmine presence.
Flavor Components
Floral Dominance: Prominent jasmine flavor that should be naturally sweet and refreshing rather than cloying
Tea Base Foundation: Underlying notes from the base tea - vegetal for green tea, honeyed for white tea, or malty for black tea
Natural Sweetness: Inherent sweetness from both the jasmine flowers and the tea leaves, often with honey-like notes
Clean Finish: Refreshing aftertaste that leaves the palate feeling clean with lingering floral notes
Balanced Bitterness: Minimal astringency that provides structure without overwhelming the delicate floral character
Mouthfeel and Texture
Jasmine tea typically has a light to medium body with a smooth, silky texture. The floral oils create a slight coating sensation on the palate that enhances the lingering aromatic experience. Well-made jasmine tea feels clean and refreshing in the mouth, with no cloying sweetness or artificial aftertaste. The balance between tea base and floral notes creates a harmonious drinking experience.

‿̩͙⊱༒︎━━━━━━﹙Health Benefits and Nutritional Profiles﹚━━━━━━༒︎⊰‿̩͙

Relaxation and Stress Relief
Jasmine tea is renowned for its calming properties, both from the L-theanine in the tea leaves and the aromatherapeutic effects of jasmine fragrance. Studies suggest that the scent of jasmine can reduce heart rate and promote feelings of calmness. The combination makes jasmine tea an excellent choice for relaxation and stress reduction.
Antioxidant Properties
Jasmine tea provides the antioxidant benefits of its base tea combined with additional compounds from jasmine flowers. The tea contains catechins, particularly when green tea is used as the base, which help combat oxidative stress. Regular consumption supports cellular health and may reduce the risk of chronic diseases associated with oxidative damage.
Digestive Support
Jasmine tea has traditionally been consumed after meals in China to aid digestion. The tea helps stimulate digestive enzymes and can relieve feelings of fullness. The gentle nature of well-made jasmine tea makes it suitable for people with sensitive stomachs, while still providing the digestive benefits of tea.
Oral Health Benefits
The antibacterial properties of both tea and jasmine flowers support oral health. Jasmine tea can help reduce bacteria associated with plaque formation and bad breath. The natural fluoride content in tea leaves contributes to dental health, while the refreshing quality of jasmine makes it an excellent natural breath freshener.
Metabolic Support
When made with green tea base, jasmine tea provides metabolic benefits including enhanced fat oxidation and thermogenesis. The combination of caffeine and catechins can support healthy weight management when combined with balanced diet and exercise. The tea's appealing flavor encourages regular consumption as part of a healthy lifestyle.

‿̩͙⊱༒︎━━━━━━﹙Traditional Brewing Methods﹚━━━━━━༒︎⊰‿̩͙

Water Temperature Precision
Jasmine tea requires careful temperature control to preserve its delicate aromas. For green tea-based jasmine, use water at 75-80°C (167-176°F). White tea bases benefit from slightly cooler water at 70-75°C (158-167°F), while black tea bases can handle hotter water up to 90°C (194°F). Water that's too hot will damage the floral notes and create bitterness.
Glassware Appreciation
Jasmine tea is ideally brewed in glass vessels to fully appreciate the visual aspects, especially when using jasmine pearls that dramatically unfurl. Glass teapots or tall glasses allow observation of the "tea dance" as leaves expand and release their fragrance. The transparent vessels also showcase the beautiful pale liquor color characteristic of quality jasmine tea.
Multiple Infusion Technique
High-quality jasmine tea can yield 2-3 excellent infusions, each revealing different aspects:

First infusion (2-3 minutes): Maximum jasmine aroma with subtle tea base notes
Second infusion (1-2 minutes): Balanced flavor with more prominent tea character
Third infusion (3-4 minutes): Subtle floral notes with clean, refreshing finish
The jasmine fragrance may diminish with subsequent infusions while the tea base becomes more prominent.
Gaiwan Brewing Method
Traditional Chinese gaiwan brewing is ideal for appreciating jasmine tea's complexity. Use 3-5 grams of leaves in a 150ml gaiwan with short steep times of 30-60 seconds for multiple infusions. This method allows precise control over extraction and fully reveals the tea's aromatic evolution through successive brews. The lidded bowl also concentrates the jasmine aroma.
Cold Brew Adaptation
Jasmine tea makes exceptional cold-brewed tea that highlights its natural sweetness and aromatic qualities. Use 10-15 grams of leaves per liter of cold water and steep in the refrigerator for 6-8 hours. Cold brewing minimizes any potential bitterness while maximizing the refreshing floral character, creating a perfect warm-weather beverage.

‿̩͙⊱༒︎━━━━━━﹙Cultural Significance and Traditions﹚━━━━━━༒︎⊰‿̩͙

Chinese Hospitality Symbol
In China, jasmine tea is traditionally served to guests as a gesture of welcome and respect. The tea's beautiful aroma and refined character make it ideal for hospitality occasions. Serving jasmine tea to visitors represents the host's good taste and care for their guests' comfort and enjoyment, embodying traditional Chinese values of hospitality.
Northern China Preference
Jasmine tea has historically been particularly popular in northern China, including Beijing, where the water is harder and more mineral-rich. The strong floral aroma of jasmine tea stands up well to the mineral character of northern water, making it more appealing than more delicate teas that might be overwhelmed by the water quality.
Literary and Artistic Associations
Jasmine tea has long been associated with Chinese literature, poetry, and art. Scholars and artists appreciated its refined character and inspirational qualities. The tea frequently appears in classical Chinese poetry and paintings as a symbol of purity, elegance, and the appreciation of life's simple pleasures. This artistic association continues in contemporary Chinese culture.
Global Tea Introduction
For many people worldwide, jasmine tea serves as their first introduction to Chinese tea culture. Its approachable floral character provides an accessible entry point to exploring more traditional Chinese teas. As such, jasmine tea has become an ambassador of Chinese tea culture internationally, representing the sophistication and variety of Chinese tea traditions while remaining welcoming to new enthusiasts.
