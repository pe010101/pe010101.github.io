Embark on a Tea Odyssey

❖

Tea's journey began in ancient China, where legend tells of Emperor Shen Nong discovering the refreshing brew in 2737 BCE when leaves serendipitously fell into his boiling water. For centuries, tea remained China's treasured secret, cultivated in misty mountains and celebrated in poetry and art.

During the Tang Dynasty, tea culture flourished with Lu Yu's "Classic of Tea," transforming preparation into an art form. Buddhist monks carried tea to Japan in the 9th century, where it evolved into the spiritual practice of Chanoyu. Meanwhile, along the Silk Road, tea began its global journey.

The 17th century saw tea reach European shores through Portuguese and Dutch traders. In Britain, it sparked social revolution with afternoon tea traditions, while in America, it fueled political change during the Boston Tea Party. Today, this ancient leaf continues to unite cultures worldwide in shared moments of tranquility and connection.
